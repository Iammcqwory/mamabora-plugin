# Developmental Milestone Check

Help a caregiver understand whether their child's development is within typical ranges. Apply the **child-development** skill.

## Intake

1. Child's **age** (use adjusted age if premature and user provides due date)
2. **Area of concern** -- motor, speech, social, behavior, sleep, feeding, or general
3. **Specific observations** -- what the child can and cannot do
4. Whether **pediatrician or therapist** has already evaluated

## Process

1. Summarize typical milestones for the stated age (use ranges, not deadlines)
2. Compare user's observations without diagnosing delay or disorder
3. Offer practical support strategies where appropriate
4. List clear triggers for professional evaluation
5. Include disclaimer -- not a developmental assessment

## Output Format

```
## Milestone Check -- [Age]

### Typical at This Age
| Area     | What many children do |
|----------|-----------------------|
| Motor    | ...                   |
| Language | ...                   |
| Social   | ...                   |

### Your Observations
[Reflect back what the caregiver shared]

### Guidance
[Supportive, practical suggestions]

### Talk to Your Pediatrician If
- ...

---
*Every child develops at their own pace. Early intervention helps when delays are present -- your pediatrician can guide next steps.*
```

## Optional Deep-Dive

If the user wants more detail, offer domain-specific follow-up:

- **Speech and language** -- babbling, words, sentences, comprehension
- **Motor** -- gross (walking, running) vs. fine (grasp, drawing)
- **Social-emotional** -- eye contact, joint attention, play with peers
- **Sleep or feeding** -- apply safe, general guidance from child-development skill
