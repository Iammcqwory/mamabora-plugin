# Symptom Assessment Skill

Use this skill for any symptom-based query after clearing the pediatric-safety red-flag screen.

## Triage Levels

| Level       | Meaning                                      | Action                                      |
|-------------|----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Emergency   | Life-threatening signs present               | Call emergency services / go to ER now      |
| Urgent      | Needs same-day medical review                | Call pediatrician or go to urgent care today |
| Non-urgent  | Can wait 24-48 hours if stable               | Schedule pediatrician appointment           |
| Self-care   | Manageable at home with monitoring           | Home care + clear return precautions        |

## Common Symptom Frameworks

### Fever

**Classify by age first:**

- Under 3 months: any fever >= 38.0 C -- Emergency
- 3-6 months: fever >= 38.0 C -- Urgent
- 6 months - 2 years: fever >= 39.0 C or lasting > 48 hours -- Urgent
- Over 2 years: fever >= 40.0 C or lasting > 3 days -- Urgent

**Self-care guidance (when non-urgent):**
- Dress lightly, maintain fluids
- Paracetamol or ibuprofen (age/weight-appropriate) -- confirm dose with pharmacist
- Monitor for red flags: rash, stiff neck, difficulty breathing, not improving

### Respiratory Symptoms

**Escalate (Urgent or Emergency) if:**
- Respiratory rate > 50/min in infants, > 40/min in toddlers, > 30/min in older children
- Retractions (skin pulling in between ribs or at neck with each breath)
- Nasal flaring
- Grunting
- Stridor (high-pitched noise on inhale)
- Cyanosis (blue lips or fingernails)
- Cannot complete sentences in older children

**Self-care guidance (mild URI, non-urgent):**
- Saline nasal drops for congestion in infants
- Elevate head of bed slightly for children over 1 year
- Steam or cool-mist humidifier
- Honey for cough in children over 1 year (never under 12 months)
- Avoid OTC cough/cold medications under 6 years

### Gastrointestinal Symptoms

**Escalate if:**
- Signs of dehydration: no wet nappies/urine in 8+ hours, no tears, sunken eyes, dry mouth
- Blood in stool beyond minor streaks
- Bile-green vomiting (suggests obstruction -- Emergency)
- Abdominal rigidity or severe localised pain
- Vomiting after head injury

**Self-care guidance (mild gastroenteritis):**
- Oral rehydration solution (ORS) -- small, frequent sips
- Continue breastfeeding if applicable
- Avoid fruit juice and sugary drinks
- BRAT diet (banana, rice, applesauce, toast) -- optional, not mandatory
- Return precaution: if not keeping fluids down for > 4 hours, seek care

### Rash

**Escalate if:**
- Non-blanching (purple/red spots that do not fade under a glass) -- Emergency (possible meningococcal)
- Widespread hives with breathing difficulty or swelling -- Emergency (anaphylaxis)
- Fever with rash in infant under 3 months -- Emergency

**Self-care guidance (mild rashes):**
- Note location, spread, and whether itchy or painful
- Avoid scratching; trim fingernails
- Cool compress for itchy rashes
- Photograph rash to show pediatrician
- Seek care if rash spreads rapidly or child becomes unwell

### Ear and Eye Concerns

**Ear:**
- Ear pulling in infants is non-specific -- only significant with fever or irritability
- Discharge from ear canal -- Urgent
- Suspected foreign body -- Urgent

**Eye:**
- Sticky eye in newborns -- common; warm compress, review if not clearing
- Conjunctivitis with significant swelling of eyelid or visual disturbance -- Urgent
- Chemical exposure to eye -- Emergency, flush immediately and seek care

## Assessment Output Checklist

For every symptom assessment, confirm the response includes:

- [ ] Age of child stated
- [ ] Triage level assigned and explained
- [ ] Non-diagnostic explanation of what may be happening
- [ ] Specific home care steps (if Self-care or Non-urgent)
- [ ] Return precautions (when to escalate)
- [ ] Disclaimer included
