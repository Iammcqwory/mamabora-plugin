# Child Development Skill

Use this skill for milestone questions, sleep, feeding, and behavior concerns.

## Important Framing

Always communicate:

- Milestones are **ranges**, not deadlines
- Premature infants use **adjusted age** until at least 2 years
- Concern is valid -- early evaluation is always better than waiting
- This skill supports conversation; it does not replace developmental screening tools (e.g. ASQ-3, M-CHAT)

## Developmental Milestone Reference

### 2 Months
- **Motor**: Holds head up briefly; pushes up when on tummy
- **Language**: Coos and makes gurgling sounds
- **Social**: Smiles responsively; briefly follows faces

### 4 Months
- **Motor**: Holds head steady; brings hands to mouth; rolls front to back
- **Language**: Babbles; laughs
- **Social**: Recognises familiar faces; interested in surroundings

### 6 Months
- **Motor**: Sits with support; transfers objects hand to hand
- **Language**: Strings vowels together; responds to name
- **Social**: Likes to look at self in mirror; shows curiosity

### 9 Months
- **Motor**: Sits without support; crawls; pulls to stand
- **Language**: Understands "no"; says "mama/dada" non-specifically
- **Social**: Stranger anxiety begins; enjoys peek-a-boo

### 12 Months
- **Motor**: Stands alone briefly; walks with support or independently
- **Language**: 1-3 words with meaning; understands simple instructions
- **Social**: Waves bye-bye; shows objects to caregivers; points

### 18 Months
- **Motor**: Walks well; begins to run; stacks 2-4 blocks
- **Language**: 10-25 words; points to body parts when named
- **Social**: Parallel play; follows 2-step instructions; imitates chores

### 24 Months
- **Motor**: Runs; kicks ball; walks up stairs with support
- **Language**: 50+ words; 2-word phrases; 50% speech intelligible to strangers
- **Social**: Plays alongside others; shows defiance (normal); symbolic play

### 3 Years
- **Motor**: Pedals tricycle; climbs; draws a circle
- **Language**: 3-4 word sentences; 75% intelligible; knows full name
- **Social**: Takes turns; plays with others; separates from caregiver more easily

### 4 Years
- **Motor**: Hops on one foot; catches ball; uses scissors
- **Language**: Tells stories; uses future tense; 100% intelligible
- **Social**: Cooperative play; tells "tall tales" (normal); distinguishes fantasy vs reality (emerging)

### 5 Years
- **Motor**: Skips; draws person with 6+ parts; writes some letters
- **Language**: Uses complex sentences; asks meaning of words
- **Social**: Wants to please; gender identity solidifying; follows rules in games

## When to Refer for Evaluation

Advise caregiver to see pediatrician promptly if:

- No smiling or facial expressions by 2 months
- Not watching faces or tracking movement by 2 months
- No babbling by 6 months
- Not responding to name by 9 months
- No words ("mama/dada" with meaning) by 12 months
- No pointing or waving by 12 months
- No 2-word phrases by 24 months
- Loss of any previously acquired language or social skills at any age
- Significant concern about hearing, vision, or social connection

## Sleep Guidance (Age-Based)

| Age               | Total sleep per 24 hours | Notes                                    |
|-------------------|--------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Newborn (0-3 mo)  | 14-17 hours              | No set schedule; frequent night waking normal |
| 4-12 months       | 12-16 hours              | Naps 2-3 times/day; night waking still common |
| 1-2 years         | 11-14 hours              | 1-2 naps; night waking decreasing        |
| 3-5 years         | 10-13 hours              | Nap may drop after age 3                 |
| 6-12 years        | 9-12 hours               | No nap; consistent bedtime important     |
| 13-18 years       | 8-10 hours               | Biological shift toward later sleep onset |

**Safe sleep (infants under 12 months):**
- Back to sleep, every sleep
- Firm, flat surface; no pillows, bumpers, or loose bedding
- Room-sharing without bed-sharing recommended for first 6 months
- Avoid overheating; use a sleep sack not a blanket

## Feeding Guidance

### Breastfeeding
- Exclusive breastfeeding recommended for first 6 months (WHO)
- Continue alongside solids through 2 years or beyond
- Cluster feeding in first weeks is normal -- not a sign of low supply
- Seek lactation support early if pain or latch difficulties persist

### Formula Feeding
- Use iron-fortified infant formula for first 12 months
- Do not dilute formula -- follow preparation instructions exactly
- Introduce cow's milk after 12 months (full fat until age 2)

### Introducing Solids (from 6 months)
- Start single-ingredient purees; wait 3 days between new foods to watch for reactions
- Common allergens (egg, peanut, fish) can be introduced early -- current evidence supports this
- No honey under 12 months (botulism risk)
- No whole grapes, nuts, or hard raw vegetables under 4 years (choking risk)
- No added salt or sugar in first year
- Baby-led weaning is safe when child has appropriate motor readiness

## Behavior Guidance

### Toddler Tantrums (18 months - 4 years)
- Normal developmental stage; driven by emotional dysregulation
- Stay calm; ensure safety; avoid reasoning mid-tantrum
- Validate feelings after calm: "You were really upset."
- Consistent routines and predictable transitions reduce frequency

### Screen Time
- Under 18 months: video chat only (not solo screen time)
- 18-24 months: high-quality programming with caregiver co-viewing
- 2-5 years: limit to 1 hour per day of high-quality content
- 6+ years: consistent limits; prioritise sleep, activity, and social time

### Discipline
- Positive reinforcement is more effective than punishment
- Time-out: 1 minute per year of age, in a safe and boring space
- Never use physical punishment -- associated with harm, not improved behaviour
- Refer for support if behaviour significantly impairs family functioning or schooling
